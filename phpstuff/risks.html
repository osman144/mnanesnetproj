Risks

This is a big deal. But what you want are “the specific risks of the study”, not “the expected regular risks of the operation that you are studying”.

  • If you are looking at two different ways of measuring body temperature in patients having craniotomies, the risk of “neurologic complications” IS NOT a risk of the study (unless, I suppose, you were sticking a temperature device in the brain itself).
  • This is one reason for the comments about “routine care” noted above. If you’re measuring blood pressure via a routinely place arterial catheter, then the possible complications of placing an arterial catheter ARE NOT a risk of the study.
