Consent

  • Anything that involves randomly assigning patients to two groups – even if the two treatments are in routine use – requires consent. Doing anything to a patient that isn’t “routine” or widely accepted requires consent UNLESS it’s clearly for quality assurance purposes (gets tricky).
  • Having patients undergo even a totally risk-free “special” exam preop or postop (e.g. neurocognitive testing) requires consent, even if there is no alteration in treatment. The key is “special”.
